Topics

Programming Languages: What, Why, & How?

  • • Concepts

    • What are Programming Languages? syntax vs semantics, interpreters vs compilers, expressive power vs Turing tarpit, safety and security, formal vs informal specifications
    • What Can a Computer Do? limits of static analysis, the full employment theorem for compiler writers, the halting problem

  • • Reading

    • Concepts in Programming Languages, Chapters 1 & 2
    • Operational Semantics, Chapter 1

Induction: The Workhorse of Programming Languages

  • • Concepts

    • Induction Primer: inductive sets and trees, proof by induction
    • Syntax & Grammars: context-free grammar, BNF, abstract vs concrete syntax trees

  • • Reading

    • Concepts in Programming Languages, Chapter 4.1
    • Programming Languages and Lambda Calculi, Chapter 2
    • Operational Semantics, Chapters 2 & 3

  • • Exercises

    • Assignment 1 DUE: January 31

Operational Semantics: Big & Small

  • • Concepts

    • Big-Step Operational Semantics: also known as "natural semantics", evaluation, derivation trees
    • Small-Step Operational Semantics: reduction rules, evaluation contexts

  • • Reading

    • Operational Semantics, Chapters 4 & 5

  • • Exercises

    • Assignment 2 DUE: February 2

Lambda Calculus: The Laws of Functions

  • • Concepts

    • Alpha, Beta, Eta: syntax of lambda calculus, variable names and alpha-equivalence, substitution and beta-equivalence, extensionality and eta-equivalence
    • Lambda Calculus in Small Steps: beta-reduction, call-by-value versus call-by-name, normalization, confluence
    • Lambda Calculus in Big Steps: evaluation order, normal forms, weak versus strong evaluation

  • • Reading

    • Concepts in Programming Languages, Chapter 4.2
    • Programming Languages and Lambda Calculi, Chapters 4.1 & 4.2

  • • Exercises

    • Assignment 3 DUE: February 9

Object-Oriented Programming

  • • Concepts

    • Introduction to Ruby: basic Ruby programming, variables and values, function definitions, interactive interpreter
    • Classes & Objects: user-defined classes, objects and encapsulation, method calls, dynamic dispatch
    • Compositional Interpreters: class-based representation of syntax trees, evaluation and interpretation methods, compositionality

  • • Notes

    • Tour of Ruby (interactive Ruby script)
